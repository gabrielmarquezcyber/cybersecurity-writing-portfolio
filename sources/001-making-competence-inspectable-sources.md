## Sources and notes

[^cyber-demand]: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, ["Information Security Analysts"](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/information-security-analysts.htm), Occupational Outlook Handbook, projects 29 percent employment growth from 2024 to 2034 and about 16,000 annual openings. CyberSeek, [Cybersecurity Supply and Demand Heat Map](https://www.cyberseek.org/heatmap.html), provides workforce supply/demand data. Fortinet, ["2026 Global Cybersecurity Skills Gap Report"](https://www.fortinet.com/content/dam/fortinet/assets/reports/2026-cybersecurity-skills-gap-report.pdf) and related [press release](https://www.fortinet.com/corporate/about-us/newsroom/press-releases/2026/fortinet-report-reveals-cybersecurity-hiring-stalls-as-nearly-half-of-it-leaders-face-corporate-pushback), is used for the senior-skill/headcount-friction framing.

[^manufacturing-retail-csr]: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, ["Forty years of falling manufacturing employment"](https://www.bls.gov/opub/btn/volume-9/forty-years-of-falling-manufacturing-employment.htm), gives the June 1979 manufacturing peak of 19.6 million and June 2019 level of 12.8 million. BLS, ["Employment Projections: 2024-2034 Summary"](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ecopro.nr0.htm), notes that retail trade is projected to lose the most jobs of any sector. BLS, ["Customer Service Representatives"](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/office-and-administrative-support/customer-service-representatives.htm), is used for the projected decline in customer-service representative employment.

[^creative-destruction]: Joseph A. Schumpeter, *Capitalism, Socialism and Democracy* (1942), source of the creative-destruction frame. For a concise reference summary, see Econlib, ["Creative Destruction"](https://www.econlib.org/library/Enc/CreativeDestruction.html).

[^autor-dorn-hanson]: David Autor, David Dorn, and Gordon Hanson, ["The China Syndrome: Local Labor Market Effects of Import Competition in the United States"](https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/aer.103.6.2121), *American Economic Review* 103, no. 6 (2013): 2121-2168. The paper is used for the claim that trade shocks and economic change can concentrate harm by geography and worker category.

[^post-2008]: Federal Reserve Bank of New York, [large-scale asset-purchase archive](https://www.newyorkfed.org/markets/programs-archive/large-scale-asset-purchases), supports the quantitative-easing reference. U.S. Treasury, [Troubled Asset Relief Program overview](https://home.treasury.gov/data/troubled-asset-relief-program), supports the bailout/TARP reference. Britannica, ["Occupy Wall Street"](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Occupy-Wall-Street), is used for the post-2008 protest-atmosphere reference.

[^graduate-underemployment]: Burning Glass Institute, ["No Country for Young Grads"](https://www.burningglassinstitute.org/research/no-country-for-young-grads), frames the weakening degree-to-professional-employment conversion. Strada Institute and Burning Glass Institute, ["Talent Disrupted"](https://www.strada.org/reports/talent-disrupted), reports that 52 percent of graduates are underemployed one year after graduation and 45 percent remain underemployed a decade later. Federal Reserve Bank of New York, ["The Labor Market for Recent College Graduates"](https://www.newyorkfed.org/research/college-labor-market), reported 41.5 percent underemployment for recent graduates in 2026 Q1.

[^participation-ticket]: Michael W. Green, ["Part 1: My Life Is a Lie"](https://www.yesigiveafig.com/p/part-1-my-life-is-a-lie), is used as a contested participation-cost frame, not as a settled poverty measure. For critical context, see Close Up Foundation, ["Should the Federal Poverty Line Be $140,000?"](https://www.closeup.org/should-the-federal-poverty-line-be-140000/) and Cato Institute, ["The $140,000 'Poverty Line' Is Laughably Wrong, So Why Does It Feel Right?"](https://www.cato.org/commentary/140000-poverty-line-laughably-wrong-so-why-does-it-feel-right).

[^best-buy-geek-squad]: Retail Dive, ["Best Buy cutting 400 remote Geek Squad jobs"](https://www.retaildive.com/news/best-buy-cutting-400-remote-geek-squad-jobs/448233/), and the Minneapolis *Star Tribune*, ["Best Buy eliminating 400 remote positions; employees will be offered other jobs"](https://www.startribune.com/best-buy-eliminating-400-remote-positions-employees-will-be-offered-other-jobs/437234663), support the 2017 remote Geek Squad outsourcing claim.

[^global-labor]: U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services, ["Characteristics of H-1B Specialty Occupation Workers, Fiscal Year 2025"](https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/data/fy25_h1b_characteristics_congress_signed_04242026.pdf), reports that computer-related occupations were the largest major occupational category among approved H-1B petitions in FY2025, accounting for 62 percent.

[^legibility]: James C. Scott, [*Seeing Like a State: How Certain Schemes to Improve the Human Condition Have Failed*](https://yalebooks.yale.edu/book/9780300078152/seeing-like-a-state/) (Yale University Press, 1998). The essay adapts Scott's legibility lens to hiring systems.

[^attention-scarcity]: Herbert A. Simon, ["Designing Organizations for an Information-Rich World"](https://www.econbiz.de/Record/designing-organizations-for-an-information-rich-world-simon-herbert/10002817747), in *Computers, Communications, and the Public Interest* (Johns Hopkins Press, 1971), is used for the attention-scarcity frame.

[^spectacle]: Guy Debord, *The Society of the Spectacle* (1967), used as a lens for image-mediated proof and professional self-display. For a concise reference summary, see ["The Society of the Spectacle"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Society_of_the_Spectacle).

[^ai-exposure]: IMF, ["AI Will Transform the Global Economy. Let's Make Sure It Benefits Humanity"](https://www.imf.org/en/blogs/articles/2024/01/14/ai-will-transform-the-global-economy-lets-make-sure-it-benefits-humanity), estimates about 60 percent of jobs in advanced economies are exposed to AI. Goldman Sachs, ["How Will AI Affect the US Labor Market?"](https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/how-will-ai-affect-the-us-labor-market), summarizes its 300-million-jobs exposure estimate. World Economic Forum, [*Future of Jobs Report 2025*](https://www.weforum.org/publications/the-future-of-jobs-report-2025/), supports the job-churn frame. McKinsey Global Institute, ["Generative AI and the future of work in America"](https://www.mckinsey.com/mgi/our-research/generative-ai-and-the-future-of-work-in-america), estimates additional occupational transitions by 2030.

[^tainter]: Joseph A. Tainter, *The Collapse of Complex Societies* (Cambridge University Press, 1988), used for the diminishing-returns-to-complexity frame. For a concise discussion, see Niskanen Center, ["The possible relevance of Joseph Tainter"](https://www.niskanencenter.org/the-possible-relevance-of-joseph-tainter/).

[^frankfurt]: Harry G. Frankfurt, *On Bullshit* (Princeton University Press, 2005), used for language that becomes indifferent to truth. Princeton Public Library's event page, ["Twenty Years of Harry Frankfurt's 'On Bullshit'"](https://princetonlibrary.libnet.info/event/14731315), provides a stable reference summary.

[^graeber]: David Graeber, [*Bullshit Jobs: A Theory*](https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/295446/bullshit-jobs-by-graeber-david/9780141983479) (2018), used for the institutional-ritual/work-meaning frame.

[^work-samples]: Philip L. Roth, Philip Bobko, and Lynn A. McFarland, ["A Meta-Analysis of Work Sample Test Validity"](https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/645/articles/a%20meta-analysis%20of%20work%20sample%20test.pdf), *Personnel Psychology* 58 (2005): 1009-1037. See also Frank L. Schmidt and John E. Hunter, ["The Validity and Utility of Selection Methods in Personnel Psychology"](https://homepages.se.edu/cvonbergen/files/2013/11/The-Validity-and-Utility-of-Selection-Methods-in-Personnel-Psychology_Practical-and-Theoretical-Implications-of-85-Years-of-Research-Findings.pdf), *Psychological Bulletin* 124, no. 2 (1998): 262-274.

[^hirschman]: Albert O. Hirschman, [*Exit, Voice, and Loyalty: Responses to Decline in Firms, Organizations, and States*](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674276604) (Harvard University Press, 1970).

[^skin-in-the-game]: Nassim Nicholas Taleb, [*Skin in the Game: Hidden Asymmetries in Daily Life*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/537828/skin-in-the-game-by-nassim-nicholas-taleb/) (Random House, 2018), used for the exposed-judgment/risk-bearing frame.
